# Use Brain in Claude (web, desktop & mobile)

Beyond the CLI plugin, you can reach your brain from the **Claude apps** — web, desktop, Cowork,
and your **phone** — through the **Brain connector**, a remote [MCP](https://modelcontextprotocol.io)
server hosted by Brain Cloud. Add it once on the web and it syncs to every Claude client on your account.

<Callout type="info">
  **Beta — read-only for now.** The connector exposes two tools today: **Recall memories** and
  **Brain status**. Adding memories from the apps (write) is coming next; for now you *create*
  memories with the CLI plugin and they appear in the apps after a sync.
</Callout>

## Before you start

You need a [Brain Cloud](https://app.brainmemory.ai) account and your brain pushed to it:

```bash
brain cloud login     # sign in (use the Google account you'll use in Claude)
brain cloud push      # upload ~/.brain to the cloud
```

<Callout type="warning">
  **Use one identity.** The connector serves the brain of whichever **Google account** you sign
  into Claude with. Make sure that's the **same account** you logged into Brain Cloud with above —
  otherwise the connector will open an empty brain.
</Callout>

## Add the connector

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the connector settings">
    On **claude.ai** (web), go to **Settings → Connectors → Add custom connector**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the URL">
    Name it `Brain` and paste the server URL:

    ```
    https://mcp.brainmemory.ai/mcp
    ```

    Leave **OAuth Client ID / Secret blank** — the connector registers Claude automatically.
    Click **Add**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Claude opens a sign-in page → **Sign in with Google** with the account that holds your brain →
    approve. The connector now shows two tools: **Recall memories** and **Brain status**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use it (incl. your phone)">
    Connectors sync to all your Claude clients — open Claude on your **iPhone/Android** and it's
    already there. Ask, for example:

    > *"Use Brain to recall what I decided about X."*

    Claude calls the tool and surfaces your real memories. If it answers from general knowledge
    instead, nudge it: *"use the Brain connector to recall…"*.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Keeping it current

The connector pulls your brain when you sign in and caches it. When you add new memories locally,
run `brain cloud push` (or `/brain:memorize --sync`) so the apps see them. Write-from-anywhere
(Phase 2) will close this loop automatically.

## Troubleshooting

- **"Server not found" when signing in** — your browser cached an old DNS answer; quit/reopen the
  browser (or clear its DNS cache) and retry the connect.
- **Recall looks empty** — you're likely signed into Claude with a different Google account than the
  one holding your brain, or a `brain cloud push` is due.
- **Claude won't use it** — say *"use the Brain connector"* explicitly.

## Prefer no cloud?

The connector requires Brain Cloud (it's how the apps reach your brain). If you'd rather avoid a
hosted service entirely, the CLI plugin + [portable sync](/docs/advanced/portable-sync/) (git, any
synced folder, or encrypted export) keep your brain across machines with no account.
